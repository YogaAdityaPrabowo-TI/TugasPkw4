Channa is a family name for snakehead fish, yes, the name of a fish that is commonly served as a meal for some people. The term channa refers to the scientific name of the family Channidae. Just like betta, chana fish are also territorial and attack the same sex who enter their territory. Therefore, channa fish are generally kept alone in one aquarium. So far it has been said that channa fish have around 50 species spread across Asia to Africa. The genus has a wide natural distribution that stretches from Iraq in the west, to Indonesia and China in east, as well as parts of Siberia in the far East. Very high species richness exists in Myanmar (Burma) and India, many species of Channa do not live anywhere else.
